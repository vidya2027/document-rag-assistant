Medical imaging systems create visual representations of internal anatomy for clinical review. Common
modalities include computed tomography, magnetic resonance imaging, ultrasound, and X-ray imaging.

Artificial intelligence can support medical imaging workflows in tasks such as image segmentation,
classification, detection, measurement, and workflow prioritization. Segmentation assigns pixels or voxels
to anatomical structures or regions of interest. Detection systems attempt to identify candidate findings
or objects in an image.

Medical imaging AI systems require careful validation. Performance should be evaluated on representative
data and relevant clinical use cases. Dataset shift, image acquisition differences, device variation, and
population differences may affect model performance.

Human oversight is important in safety-critical healthcare workflows. An AI output should be interpreted
within the intended use of the system and the applicable clinical process. Monitoring, traceability, data
governance, and validation are important engineering considerations.
